\begin{center}
{\Large\bfseries Catching IGM Dust in the Act:}\\[2pt]
{\large\bfseries A Per-Sightline UV Spectroscopic Test with HST/STIS and SNe Ia}
\end{center}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent
Type~Ia supernovae (SNe~Ia) anchor modern cosmology, yet Stage~IV
systematics now dominate their distance budget. The Union3 analysis
identifies intergalactic dust extinction as the second-largest SNe~Ia
systematic --- yet this dust has only ever been inferred
\emph{statistically}, through cross-correlation of quasar colors with
foreground galaxies (M\'enard et~al.\ 2010). In the sixteen years
since, no study has directly tied \ion{Mg}{2} absorption to reddening
along any individual SNe~Ia sightline.

\begin{sloppypar}
We propose a 35-orbit non-disruptive ToO program to obtain
HST/STIS~G230MB (CENWAVE~3115) NUV spectroscopy of 5 anomalously
reddened SNe~Ia at $z=0.3$--0.5 in the COSMOS Deep Drilling Field,
triggered within three weeks of LSST/Rubin discovery. A five-stage
pipeline --- LSST alert; COSMOS2020 photo-$z=0.30$--0.50; dDLR$>3$ for
host-dust rejection; a foreground galaxy at $z_{\rm fg}\sim0.1$ within
50~kpc; and SALT3 $\Delta E(B{-}V)\geq0.03$~mag --- yields 5--8
STIS-ready targets per year. At $z_{\rm fg}\sim0.1$,
\ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796,~2803 lies at observed
3076--3083~\AA, below the atmospheric cutoff and accessible only from
space. Each target needs 4--8 orbits for S/N~$\geq20$ per resel.
\end{sloppypar}

A single detection correlated with the photometric reddening would
constitute the \emph{first direct, per-sightline spectroscopic
evidence} that IGM dust biases the SNe~Ia Hubble diagram --- converting
a 16-year statistical inference into a confirmed cosmological
systematic. A null across 5 sightlines places a $2\sigma$ constraint
on the M\'enard prediction, equally informative for Stage~IV
cosmology. G230MB is the only STScI-supported medium-resolution mode
in this NUV band in Cycle~34, making HST uniquely capable of the
measurement.
