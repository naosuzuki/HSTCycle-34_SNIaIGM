\noindent
Type~Ia supernovae (SNe~Ia) anchor modern cosmology, yet Stage~IV
systematics now dominate their distance budget. The Union3 analysis
identifies intergalactic dust extinction as the second-largest SNe~Ia
systematic --- yet this dust has only ever been inferred
\emph{statistically}, through cross-correlation of quasar colors with
foreground galaxies (M\'enard et~al.\ 2010). In the sixteen years
since, no study has obtained direct spectroscopic evidence that
reddening along any individual SNe~Ia sightline is caused by
intervening \ion{Mg}{2} absorption.

\begin{sloppypar}
We propose a 34-orbit non-disruptive ToO program to obtain HST/COS
G185M UV spectroscopy of 5--7 anomalously reddened SNe~Ia in the
COSMOS Deep Drilling Field, triggered within three weeks of
LSST/Rubin discovery. A five-stage pipeline --- LSST alert;
COSMOS2020 photo-$z=0.30$--0.45; dDLR$>3$ for host-dust rejection;
impact parameter $\rho<200$~kpc; SALT3 $\Delta E(B{-}V)\geq0.03$~mag
--- yields 6--10 COS-ready targets per year. At foreground-galaxy
redshifts we search for \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796,~2803;
sightlines placing Ly$\alpha$ in COS~G130M enable a bonus DLA search.
\end{sloppypar}

A single detection correlated with the photometric reddening would
constitute the \emph{first direct, per-sightline spectroscopic
evidence} that IGM dust biases the SNe~Ia Hubble diagram --- converting
a 16-year statistical inference into a confirmed cosmological
systematic. A null over six sightlines instead places a $2\sigma$
constraint on the M\'enard prediction, equally informative for
Stage~IV cosmology. HST/COS is uniquely required: no other facility
delivers the NUV coverage, $R\sim16{,}000$--20{,}000, and sensitivity
to detect $W_0(2796)\geq 0.3$~\AA\ at $m_{\rm UV}\sim20.5$--21.5~AB.
