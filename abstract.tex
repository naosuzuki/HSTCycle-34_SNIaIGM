\begin{center}
{\Large\bfseries Catching IGM Dust in the Act:}\\[2pt]
{\large\bfseries A Per-Sightline UV Spectroscopic Test with HST/STIS and SNe Ia}
\end{center}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent
Type~Ia supernovae (SNe~Ia) anchor modern cosmology, yet Stage~IV
systematics now dominate their distance budget. The Union3 analysis
identifies intergalactic dust extinction as the second-largest SNe~Ia
systematic, yet this dust has only ever been inferred
\emph{statistically} (M\'enard et~al.\ 2010). In the sixteen years
since, no study has directly tied \ion{Mg}{2} absorption to reddening
along an individual SNe~Ia sightline.

\begin{sloppypar}
We propose a 35-orbit non-disruptive ToO program to obtain
HST/STIS~G230MB (CENWAVE~3115) NUV spectroscopy of 5 anomalously
reddened SNe~Ia at $z=0.3$--0.5 in the COSMOS DDF, triggered within
three weeks of LSST/Rubin discovery. A five-stage pipeline (LSST
alert; photo-$z=0.30$--0.50; dDLR$>3$; foreground galaxy at
$z_{\rm fg}\sim0.1$ within 50~kpc; SALT3
$\Delta E(B{-}V)\geq0.03$~mag) yields 5--8 STIS-ready targets per
year. \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796,~2803 at
$z_{\rm fg}\sim0.1$ lies at observed 3076--3083~\AA, below the
atmospheric cutoff. Each target needs 4--8 orbits for S/N~$\geq20$
per resel.
\end{sloppypar}

A detection correlated with the photometric reddening would be the
\emph{first per-sightline evidence} that IGM dust biases the SNe~Ia
Hubble diagram --- converting a statistical inference into a
\emph{per-object} correction. A null across 5 sightlines sets a
$2\sigma$ limit on the M\'enard prediction. This program is a
\textbf{proof of concept}: it establishes the pre-selection, NUV
spectroscopy, and $W_0(2796)$--$\Delta E(B{-}V)$ calibration that
serve as the essential HST pilot for the \textbf{Roman Space
Telescope} High-Latitude Time-Domain Survey ($\sim$10{,}000 SNe~Ia
to $z\sim3$, IGM dust already the second-largest projected
systematic). G230MB is the only STScI-supported NUV medium-resolution
mode in this band in Cycle~34.
