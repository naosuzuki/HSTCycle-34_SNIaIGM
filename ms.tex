\justification % Do not delete this command.

\subsection*{Background and Motivation}

Type~Ia supernovae (SNe~Ia) are the cornerstone of modern cosmology
\citep{Riess1998, Perlmutter1999}, and in the Stage~IV era systematic
uncertainties now dominate their distance budget. The Union3 analysis
\citep{Rubin2023} identifies \emph{intergalactic dust extinction} as the
second-largest systematic in SNe~Ia cosmology --- a critical limitation
on the next generation of $w(z)$ constraints.

The existence of this dust rests entirely on \emph{statistical}
detections. For example, \citet{Menard2010} showed, via quasar-galaxy colour
cross-correlation, that IGM dust imprints a mean rest-frame $B$-band
opacity $A_B\gtrsim0.03$~mag at $z=0.5$; this result has since been
extended to megaparsec scales by \citet{McCleary2026} using a
maximum-likelihood analysis of background-galaxy reddening. The dust
lives in \ion{Mg}{2} clouds around galaxies, with
$\Omega_{\rm dust}\sim2\times10^{-6}$ and a dust-to-gas ratio
consistent with outflow origin \citep{MenardFukugita2012, LanMo2018};
stacked spectra further constrain their metallicity evolution and
$\sim$30~pc cloud sizes \citep{LanFukugita2017}. Per-sightline
predictions for SNe~Ia are given by \citet{Menard2026}. Although
\ion{Mg}{2} traces cool gas rather than dust directly, the
\citet{Menard2010} IGM-dust opacity was \emph{itself} derived by
stacking quasar colour excesses on \ion{Mg}{2} absorber positions ---
i.e., the cosmologically relevant dust signal lives in \ion{Mg}{2}
systems by construction, making \ion{Mg}{2} equivalent width the
cleanest per-sightline probe of the predicted dust column.

In the sixteen years since \citet{Menard2010}, \emph{no study has
obtained direct spectroscopic evidence} that reddening along any
individual SNe~Ia sightline is caused by intervening \ion{Mg}{2}
absorption: the statistical inference has never been verified on a
per-object basis. We pursue this first-ever per-sightline test with a
non-disruptive Target of Opportunity (ToO) program using the Space
Telescope Imaging Spectrograph (STIS).

\subsection*{Scientific Goal}

\textbf{First-ever direct spectroscopic detection of IGM \ion{Mg}{2}
absorption along SNe~Ia sightlines.} NUV spectroscopy of anomalously
reddened SNe~Ia at $z_{\rm SN}=0.3$--0.5 in the COSMOS Deep Drilling
Field (DDF) to search for \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796, 2803
imprinted by foreground galaxies at $z_{\rm fg}\approx0.1$, placing the
doublet at observed $\sim$3076--3083~\AA\ --- below the atmospheric
cutoff and accessible only from space. Detection correlated with the
photometric excess $\Delta E(B-V)$ would be the first direct,
per-object evidence that the IGM dust hypothesis of
\citet{Menard2010, Menard2026} biases the SNe~Ia Hubble diagram.

\subsection*{Why HST/STIS is Uniquely Required}

At $z_{\rm fg}\approx0.1$, the \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796, 2803
doublet falls at 3076--3083~\AA, below the $\sim$3200~\AA\ atmospheric
cutoff; no ground facility can access it at the required S/N. STIS
G230MB at CENWAVE~3115 covers 3001--3233~\AA\ at $R\sim7{,}000$ on the
CCD --- the only STScI-supported medium-resolution NUV mode in this
band (COS~NUV cannot reach 3080~\AA, and G285M is
Available-but-Unsupported in Cycle~34). The SN~Ia rest-frame continuum
at 2050--2370~\AA\ ($z_{\rm SN}=0.3$--0.5) redshifts cleanly into this
window at $m_{\rm UV}\sim21$~AB.

\subsection*{Target Field: COSMOS Deep Drilling Field}

We target the COSMOS DDF ($10^{\rm h}00^{\rm m}, +02^\circ12'$), which
uniquely combines (i) \textbf{LSST priority} --- double-visit cadence,
reaching 10-yr depth in the first 3~yr \citep{GrisDDF2024}; (ii)
\textbf{Ancillary data} --- COSMOS2020 photo-$z$ to
$\sigma_z/(1+z)\sim0.01$ to $i\sim25$~AB \citep{Weaver2022},
zCOSMOS/C3R2 spectroscopy, and HST-ACS morphology for precise dDLR;
(iii) \textbf{Contemporaneous spectroscopy} --- PFS/Subaru will obtain
$\sim$20{,}000 host redshifts during LSST. From the LSST rate and COSMOS
double allocation we expect 200--300 SNe~Ia~yr$^{-1}$ at
$z_{\rm SN}=0.3$--0.5, of which 5--8 will satisfy the full selection,
sufficient for 5 ToO triggers per Cycle.

% 2. DESCRIPTION OF THE OBSERVATIONS
\describeobservations % Do not delete this command.

\subsection*{Overview}

We request 35 HST orbits for a non-disruptive ToO program to obtain
STIS/G230MB (CENWAVE~3115) NUV spectroscopy of 5 SNe~Ia in the COSMOS
DDF, triggered within 3~weeks of LSST/Rubin discovery. Each target
needs 5--8 orbits (7 on average) to reach S/N~$\geq20$ per resel on
the \ion{Mg}{2} doublet at observed $\sim$3080~\AA.

\subsection*{Target Selection Cascade}

A five-stage pipeline pre-qualifies candidates before any HST time is
committed (Figure~\ref{fig:selection}). \textbf{(1)~LSST alert:}
Rubin-classified SNe~Ia with normal light-curve parameters.
\textbf{(2)~Photo-$z$:} COSMOS2020 host $z_{\rm SN}=0.30$--0.50 to
$\sigma_z/(1+z)<0.02$, placing the SN UV continuum in the G230MB band.
\textbf{(3)~Large dDLR:} dDLR~$>3$ \citep{Sullivan2006} removes SNe~Ia
in dusty disks ($\sim$60\% of the pool), so any residual colour excess
is plausibly IGM. \textbf{(4)~Foreground-galaxy sightline:}
COSMOS2020/zCOSMOS galaxies at $z_{\rm fg}=0.08$--0.12 with
$\rho\leq50$~kpc; MAGIICAT gives 8$\sigma$ anti-correlation of
$W_0(2796)$ with $\rho$ and $\sim$70\% covering fraction for
$W_0>0.3$~\AA\ at this $\rho$ \citep{Nielsen2013, Dutta2020}.
\textbf{(5)~Anomalous reddening:} SALT3 residual
$\Delta E(B-V)\geq0.03$~mag \citep{Menard2010, Menard2026}. The cascade
yields 5--8 STIS-ready targets per year; we trigger the top 5 per
Cycle, ranked by $\rho$ then $\Delta E(B-V)$.

\subsection*{Instrument Configuration}

All observations use STIS with the $52\arcsec\!\times\!0.2\arcsec$ slit,
G230MB/CENWAVE~3115 on the CCD, covering 3001--3233~\AA\ at
$R\sim7{,}000$ ($\Delta v\sim43$~km~s$^{-1}$/resel) --- sufficient to
detect and deblend \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda\lambda$2796, 2803 at
$W_0(2796)\geq0.3$~\AA\ from foreground galaxies at
$z_{\rm fg}=0.08$--0.12. STIS/G230MB on the CCD delivers the highest
throughput and is the only STScI-supported mode for medium-resolution
NUV spectroscopy in this band. Target acquisition uses \texttt{ACQ/PEAK}
on the SN with MIRROR in an imaging ACQ ($\sim$6~min per visit);
multi-orbit visits split the exposure into $\sim$900--1200~s
sub-exposures with CR-SPLIT and along-slit dither for cosmic-ray and
fixed-pattern mitigation. Targets at $m_{\rm UV}\sim20.5$--21.5~AB are
well below the STIS/CCD bright-object limit and will be re-checked via
the STScI ETC before each trigger.

\subsection*{Exposure Time and Orbit Budget}

We target S/N~$\geq20$/resel near 3080~\AA\ ($5\sigma$ detection of
$W_0(2796)\geq0.3$~\AA, covering the full halo-absorber population
\citep{Nielsen2013}). STIS ETC scaling for G230MB/CCD/CENWAVE~3115
gives continuum S/N/resel/orbit of $\sim$18, 11, 7, 4 at $m_{\rm UV}=$
20.0, 20.5, 21.0, 21.5~AB (2500~s science time per orbit,
0.2\arcsec\ slit):

\noindent
The baseline observes \textbf{5 targets} at $z_{\rm SN}=0.3$--0.5
consuming $\sim$30--35 orbits: 4 for the UV-brightest trigger and up to
8 for the faintest. The \textbf{35-orbit} request is a single-program
block; unused contingency from early bright triggers absorbs the
higher-$z$ tail without a revised allocation. At $<$1 trigger per month
on average, HST scheduling is not disrupted and no per-trigger approval
is required beyond TAC approval of this program.

\subsection*{Expected Outcomes and Impact}

Pre-selection on $z_{\rm fg}\approx0.1$ foreground galaxies at
$\rho<50$~kpc exploits the $\sim$70\% \ion{Mg}{2} covering fraction at
$W_0>0.3$~\AA\ \citep{Nielsen2013, Dutta2020, LanMo2018}, yielding
$\sim$3--4 expected detections across 5 sightlines.

We frame this 5-target, 35-orbit program as a \textbf{proof of
concept}. Why does a per-sightline detection matter beyond being the
first? Current SNe~Ia budgets treat IGM dust as a purely statistical
floor; a confirmed $W_0(2796)$--$\Delta E(B-V)$ correlation converts
this floor into a \emph{per-object} correction, assigning each future
SN~Ia with a foreground \ion{Mg}{2} sightline a
spectroscopically-calibrated distance prior. A null across 5
sightlines is equally informative: a $2\sigma$ limit on the
\citet{Menard2010, Menard2026} covering fraction. Either outcome makes
this the essential HST pilot for the \textbf{Roman Space Telescope}
High-Latitude Time-Domain Survey ($\sim$10{,}000 SNe~Ia to $z\sim3$,
IGM dust already the second-largest projected systematic), delivering
the pre-launch $W_0$--$\Delta E(B-V)$ calibration that Roman, Euclid,
and next-decade $w(z)$ surveys need to address the \citet{Rubin2023}
Union3 systematic on a per-object basis.

\specialreq % Do not delete this command.

This program requests \textbf{non-disruptive ToO} status with the
following requirements: (i) \textbf{Trigger window:} each execution
within 3 weeks of LSST discovery, corresponding to $\pm$2 weeks from
$B$-band maximum (required for adequate UV S/N); (ii)
\textbf{Multi-orbit visits:} 4--8 consecutive or near-consecutive
orbits per target (Table~\ref{tab:orbits}), standard guide-star
reacquisition; (iii) \textbf{CR-SPLIT and slit dither:} each orbit
split into 2--3 sub-exposures with along-slit offsets for cosmic-ray
and fixed-pattern mitigation on the STIS/CCD; (iv)
\textbf{Bright-object screening:} pre-trigger ETC check against the
STIS CCD bright-object limit; (v) \textbf{No scheduling windows}
required beyond the 3-week post-discovery constraint.

This program is scientifically coordinated with the LSST/Rubin COSMOS
DDF survey (SNe~Ia alerts and light curves for target selection) and
with the PFS/Subaru COSMOS spectroscopic survey (host-galaxy redshifts).
Both are public; no formal coordination agreement is required. No
pre-approved DD or GO HST observations in COSMOS overlap our science
goals, and the proposed STIS NUV spectroscopy has not been obtained
for any LSST-discovered transient to date.
