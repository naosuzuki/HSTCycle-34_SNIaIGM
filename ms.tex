\subsection*{Background and Motivation}

Type~Ia supernovae (SNe~Ia) are the cornerstone of modern cosmology
\citep{Riess1998, Perlmutter1999}, and in the Stage~IV era systematic
uncertainties now dominate their distance budget. The Union3 analysis
\citep{Rubin2023} identifies \emph{intergalactic dust extinction} as the
second-largest systematic in SNe~Ia cosmology --- a critical limitation
on the next generation of $w(z)$ constraints.

The existence of this dust rests entirely on \emph{statistical}
detections. \citet{Menard2010} showed, via cross-correlation of quasar
colors with foreground galaxies on 20~kpc--Mpc scales, that IGM dust
imprints a redshift-dependent dimming and reddening with mean rest-frame
$B$-band opacity $A_B \gtrsim 0.03$~mag at $z=0.5$. This dust lives in
\ion{Mg}{2} absorbing clouds around galaxies, with cosmic density
$\Omega_{\rm dust} \sim 2\times10^{-6}$ and a dust-to-gas ratio
consistent with outflow origin \citep{MenardFukugita2012}. Updated
per-sightline predictions for SNe~Ia are given by \citet{Menard2026}.

In the sixteen years since \citet{Menard2010}, \emph{no study has
obtained direct spectroscopic evidence} that reddening along any
individual SNe~Ia sightline is caused by intervening \ion{Mg}{2}
absorption --- the statistical inference has never been verified on a
per-object basis. A successful detection by this program would therefore
be the first direct, per-sightline confirmation in history that IGM dust
biases the SNe~Ia Hubble diagram. We pursue this with a non-disruptive
Target of Opportunity (ToO) program using the Cosmic Origins Spectrograph
(COS).

\subsection*{Scientific Goals}

\begin{enumerate}
\itemsep 0pt
\item \textbf{First-ever direct spectroscopic detection of IGM
  \ion{Mg}{2} absorption along SNe~Ia sightlines.} UV spectroscopy of
  anomalously reddened SNe~Ia in the COSMOS Deep Drilling Field (DDF) to
  search for \ion{Mg}{2} $\lambda\lambda$2796, 2803 at the redshifts of
  foreground galaxies on each sightline. Detection correlated with the
  photometric color excess $\Delta E(B-V)$ would provide the first
  direct, per-object evidence --- as opposed to statistical inference
  ---  that the IGM dust hypothesis of \citet{Menard2010, Menard2026}
  biases the SNe~Ia Hubble diagram.
\item \textbf{Search for Damped Ly$\alpha$ (DLA) systems.} For sightlines
  placing Ly$\alpha$ in the COS G130M band, we will search for DLA damping
  wings ($N_{\rm HI} \geq 2\times10^{20}$~cm$^{-2}$) to directly measure
  the neutral hydrogen column of the absorbing IGM dust and constrain
  its dust-to-gas ratio.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Why HST/COS is Uniquely Required}

The \ion{Mg}{2} $\lambda\lambda$2796, 2803 doublet at $z_{\rm fg}=0.1$--0.5
falls at 3080--4200~\AA, inaccessible from the ground below
$\sim$3200~\AA\ at the required sensitivity. COS~G185M is the only
facility delivering $R\sim16{,}000$--20{,}000 with the UV throughput to
reach SNe~Ia at $z\sim0.3$--0.45 ($m_{\rm UV}\sim20.5$--21.5~AB).

\subsection*{Target Field: COSMOS Deep Drilling Field}

We target the COSMOS DDF ($10^{\rm h}00^{\rm m}$, $+02^\circ12'$), which
uniquely combines: (i) \textbf{LSST priority} --- double visits of other
DDFs ($\sim$40{,}000 over 10~yr), reaching 10-yr depth in the first 3~yr
\citep{GrisDDF2024}; (ii) \textbf{Ancillary data} --- COSMOS2020
photo-$z$ to $\sigma_z/(1+z)\sim0.01$ for $\sim$$10^6$ galaxies to
$i\sim25$~AB \citep{Weaver2022}, zCOSMOS/C3R2 spectroscopy at $z<1$, and
HST-ACS morphology for precise dDLR; (iii) \textbf{Contemporaneous
spectroscopy} --- PFS/Subaru will obtain $\sim$20{,}000 host redshifts
during LSST, removing the need for pre-trigger observations.

From the LSST Science Book rate ($\sim$2{,}500 SNe~Ia DDF$^{-1}$~yr$^{-1}$)
and the COSMOS double allocation, we expect 150--250 SNe~Ia~yr$^{-1}$ at
$z=0.3$--0.45, of which 6--10 will satisfy our selection criteria ---
sufficient for 5--7 ToO triggers per Cycle.

% 2. DESCRIPTION OF THE OBSERVATIONS
\describeobservations % Do not delete this command.

\subsection*{Overview}

We request 34 HST orbits for a non-disruptive ToO program to obtain
COS/NUV G185M spectroscopy of 5--7 SNe~Ia in the COSMOS DDF, triggered
within 3~weeks of LSST/Rubin discovery. Each target needs 3--6 orbits,
within the 34-orbit small-program limit.

\subsection*{Target Selection Cascade}

A five-stage pipeline pre-qualifies candidates before any HST time is
committed (Figure~\ref{fig:selection}). \textbf{(1)~LSST alert:}
Rubin-classified SNe~Ia from the 60-s alert stream with normal
light-curve parameters. \textbf{(2)~Photo-$z$ filter:} COSMOS2020
host-$z$ in 0.30--0.45 to $\sigma_z/(1+z)<0.02$; this window places any
foreground \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda$2796 within the G185M peak-sensitivity
band (1700--2100~\AA, CENWAVE~1900) and keeps $m_{\rm UV}\lesssim21.5$.
\textbf{(3)~Large dDLR (host-dust rejection):} we require dDLR~$>3$
\citep{Sullivan2006}, rejecting SNe~Ia in dusty disks so any color
excess is plausibly of IGM origin; the HST-ACS COSMOS catalog supplies
morphology to $i\sim26$ ($\sim$60\% of the pool cut here).
\textbf{(4)~Foreground-galaxy sightline search:} COSMOS2020/zCOSMOS
galaxies at $z_{\rm fg}<z_{\rm SN}$ with $\rho\leq200$~kpc, tiered as
high ($\rho<50$) / medium (50--150~kpc); MAGIICAT shows 8$\sigma$
anti-correlation of $W_0(2796)$ with $\rho$ and $\sim$70\% covering for
$W_0>0.3$~\AA\ at $\rho<50$~kpc \citep{Nielsen2013, Dutta2020}. Sightlines
placing Ly$\alpha$ in G130M are flagged as DLA candidates.
\textbf{(5)~Anomalous reddening:} LSST SALT3 residual
$\Delta E(B-V)\geq0.03$~mag --- with host dust already removed at
Stage~3, this biases to IGM-origin sightlines \citep{Menard2010,
Menard2026}.

The full cascade yields 6--10 COS-ready targets per year; we trigger
the best 5--7 per Cycle, ranked by $\rho$ and then $\Delta E(B-V)$.

\subsection*{Instrument Configuration}

All observations use COS/PSA with grating G185M, CENWAVE~1900~\AA,
yielding continuous 1700--2100~\AA\ coverage across NUV stripes A/B/C
and reaching $R\sim16{,}000$--20{,}000 ($\Delta v\sim15$--20~km~s$^{-1}$
per resel) --- sufficient to resolve the \ion{Mg}{2} doublet and measure
$W_0(2796)\geq0.3$~\AA. All four FP-POS positions are used to mitigate
fixed-pattern noise. Target acquisition is ACQ/IMAGE with MIRRORA
($\sim$6~min per visit). Targets at $m_{\rm UV}\sim20.5$--21.5~AB are
far below the COS NUV bright-object limits and will be screened via the
STScI ETC before each trigger.

\subsection*{Exposure Time and Orbit Budget}

We target S/N~$\geq10$ per resel at the \ion{Mg}{2}~$\lambda$2796
wavelength of the foreground absorber --- sufficient to detect
$W_0(2796)\geq0.3$~\AA\ at $>3\sigma$ and cover the full halo-absorber
range \citep{Nielsen2013}. COS is a photon counter (no read noise), so
S/N$\propto\sqrt{t}$. At our nominal $z_{\rm SN}=0.35$
($m_{\rm UV}\approx21.0$~AB) the required on-source time is
$\sim$10{,}500~s; with $\sim$44~min per orbit after guide-star (re)acq
and overheads, each target needs 4 orbits.

\noindent
Our baseline observes 6 targets at $z_{\rm SN}=0.30$--0.40 in
$\sim$24--30 orbits, with 4--10 orbits held in contingency for slightly
higher-$z$ targets or scheduling reobservations. The \textbf{34-orbit}
request accommodates 5--7 targets with full flexibility. Each visit is
multi-orbit with FP-POS positions distributed across exposures.

\subsection*{Non-Disruptive ToO Designation}

Triggers are submitted only when a candidate passes the automated
five-stage pipeline, within 3 weeks of discovery and within $\pm$2 weeks
of $B$-band maximum. At $<$1 trigger per month on average, HST
scheduling is not disrupted; no per-trigger approval is required beyond
TAC approval of this program.

\subsection*{Expected Outcomes and Impact}

The strong-absorber incidence rate $dN/dz\sim0.25$ at $z\sim0.3$--0.5
\citep{MenardFukugita2012} implies $\sim$0.4--0.6 blind detections in 6
sightlines; pre-selecting on foreground galaxies at $\rho<200$~kpc
raises this to $\sim$1--2 per 6 targets \citep{Nielsen2013}. A single
detection correlated with the photometric $\Delta E(B-V)$ excess would
be the \emph{first direct spectroscopic evidence in history} that IGM
dust along an individual SNe~Ia sightline biases its inferred
distance --- converting a 16-year-old statistical inference into a
confirmed, per-object systematic. A null result over 6 sightlines
instead places a $2\sigma$ constraint on the M\'enard prediction, an
equally informative outcome for Stage~IV cosmology. DLA detection is a
bonus, directly measuring the neutral-hydrogen column of the absorber.

% 4. SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS
\specialreq % Do not delete this command.

This program requests \textbf{non-disruptive ToO} status with the
following requirements: (i) \textbf{Trigger window:} each execution
within 3 weeks of LSST discovery, corresponding to $\pm$2 weeks from
$B$-band maximum (required for adequate UV S/N); (ii)
\textbf{Multi-orbit visits:} 3--6 consecutive or near-consecutive
orbits per target (Table~\ref{tab:orbits}), standard guide-star
reacquisition; (iii) \textbf{FP-POS~1--4:} all four focal-plane offsets
used to remove fixed-pattern noise on the COS~NUV~MAMA; (iv)
\textbf{Bright-object screening:} pre-trigger ETC check against all
COS NUV limits; (v) \textbf{No scheduling windows} required beyond the
3-week post-discovery constraint.

% 5. COORDINATED OBSERVATIONS
\coordinatedobs % Do not delete this command.

This program is scientifically coordinated with the LSST/Rubin COSMOS
DDF survey (SNe~Ia alerts and light curves for target selection) and
with the PFS/Subaru COSMOS spectroscopic survey (host-galaxy redshifts).
Both are public; no formal coordination agreement is required. No
pre-approved DD or GO HST observations in COSMOS overlap our science
goals, and the proposed COS UV spectroscopy has not been obtained for
any LSST-discovered transient to date.
